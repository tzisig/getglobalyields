## What Went Wrong

The portfolio collapse was swift and devastating:

- **Starting capital**: $100,000
- **Lowest point**: $28,000
- **Drawdown**: -72%

The mistakes were predictable and repeated across many traders:

**Overleveraged options trading** — Using TQQQ as collateral for aggressive covered call positions without adequate capital reserves. One market reversal wiped out months of premium collection.

**Emotional decisions** — Panic selling at the worst possible time, locking in losses instead of averaging down during the recovery phase.

[IMAGE_2: Emotional trading illustration]

## The Turning Point

The shift came with a single decision: **stop predicting the market, start respecting risk.**

Instead of trying to time bounces or scalp 1-2% moves, the focus moved to:

- Long-term TQQQ accumulation
- Disciplined position sizing
- Options income as a *supplement*, not the main strategy

The recovery wasn't about getting lucky on one trade. It was about systematic rebuilding.

## Recovery Phase

Over the next 24 months:

- **Rebuilt position in TQQQ** — Dollar-cost averaging into every dip, treating volatility as opportunity rather than threat
- **Reduced emotional interference** — Set rules before markets opened; followed them regardless of news
- **Long-term holding approach** — Stopped checking daily P&L, focused on quarterly and annual progress

Small options income ($200-400/month from cash-secured puts and covered calls) provided reinvestment capital without betting the entire account.

[IMAGE_3: Drawdown timeline chart]

## Results

**Total invested**: $140,000 (including the original $100K)
**Current portfolio value**: $503,000

That's a 259% return on total capital deployed, or roughly +200% from the -70% low point.

## Key Lesson

Risk management always beats prediction.

The traders who survived 2022 weren't the ones who predicted the bounce correctly. They were the ones who sized positions small enough to survive being wrong, and disciplined enough to keep buying when everyone else was terrified.
