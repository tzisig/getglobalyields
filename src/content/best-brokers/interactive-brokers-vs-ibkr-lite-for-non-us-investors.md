## IBKR Lite vs IBKR Pro - What's the Difference? {#lite-vs-pro}

Interactive Brokers offers two distinct account tiers - but only one of them is available if you live outside the United States.

**IBKR Lite** was launched in 2019 as IBKR's answer to Robinhood and other zero-commission platforms. It offers commission-free trading on US stocks and ETFs for US residents. The trade-off is that it uses payment for order flow (PFOF) - meaning your orders are routed to market makers who pay for that privilege, often resulting in slightly worse execution prices. IBKR Lite also has fewer features, no access to futures or bonds, and limited platform tools.

**IBKR Pro** is the original product - the one institutions, professional traders, and serious retail investors worldwide have used for decades. It charges commissions on trades, but delivers superior order execution through SmartRouting, access to all asset classes, competitive margin rates, cash interest, and the full suite of research and trading tools.

For US residents, choosing between the two involves a real trade-off. For everyone else, there is no choice.

## Why IBKR Lite Is Not Available Outside the US {#lite-not-available}

> **The short answer: IBKR Lite does not exist for non-US investors. If you live outside the United States, you will get IBKR Pro - automatically, with no option to switch.**

This is one of the most searched questions about Interactive Brokers, and it trips up investors constantly. Here's why:

IBKR Lite is built on **payment for order flow (PFOF)** - a practice where market makers pay brokers to route customer orders to them. PFOF is legal in the US but is **banned or heavily restricted** in the EU, [UK](/us-investing/invest-us-stocks-uk), [Canada](/us-investing/invest-us-stocks-canada), [Australia](/us-investing/invest-us-stocks-australia), and many other jurisdictions. Israel, while not explicitly banning PFOF by name, falls under the category of markets where IBKR chooses not to implement it.

Interactive Brokers is regulated by more than 10 financial authorities worldwide, and maintaining compliance across all of them means IBKR Lite's business model simply can't be applied globally.

The result: if you're in Israel, [Germany](/us-investing/invest-us-stocks-germany), the UK, Canada, Singapore, Australia - or anywhere outside the US - you are an IBKR Pro customer from day one. The commission-free headlines you may have seen don't apply to you.

**Is this a problem?** Not necessarily. Read on to see why IBKR Pro is a genuinely strong product for international investors.

## IBKR for Israeli Investors - Key Details {#israeli-investors}

For Israeli residents specifically, here's what you need to know before opening an account.

### Which IBKR Entity Do Israelis Use?

Israeli residents typically open accounts with **Interactive Brokers LLC** (the US entity) - and this is generally the better choice for Israelis. Here's why it matters:

| Entity | Investor Protection | Recommended for Israelis? |
|---|---|---|
| **Interactive Brokers LLC (US)** | SIPC up to $500,000 ($250,000 cash) | **Yes - preferred** |
| Interactive Brokers Ireland | Investor Compensation up to €20,000 | No - significantly lower protection |
| Interactive Brokers UK | FSCS up to £85,000 | No - unless you're a UK resident |

The application will usually route Israeli residents to the LLC entity automatically, but it's worth confirming during the process. If you're ever offered the Ireland entity, understand that the investor protection drops from $500,000 to €20,000 - a meaningful difference for anyone with a serious portfolio. The LLC entity also gives you access to the full range of US market products with no PRIIPs restrictions.

### W-8BEN - What It Is and Why You Need It

When opening an IBKR account as an Israeli investor, you'll be required to complete a **Form W-8BEN** online - a US tax form certifying that you are not a US taxpayer. This is standard for all non-US investors and has two important effects:

- **US dividend withholding:** Without a W-8BEN, IBKR withholds 30% on US dividends. Under the Israel-US tax treaty, Israeli residents who submit a W-8BEN and claim treaty benefits are eligible for a **reduced rate of 25%** (or 15% in some qualifying cases). IBKR applies the reduced rate automatically once the form is on file.
- **Capital gains:** The US does not tax non-US persons on capital gains from US securities. You handle capital gains reporting in Israel according to Israeli law.

### Taxation for Israeli Investors

| Tax Type | Rate | Where Paid |
|---|---|---|
| Capital gains on US stocks | 25% | Israel (to the ITA - רשות המסים) |
| Dividends from US stocks | 15%-25%* | Withheld at source by IBKR |
| Interest income | 15%-25% | Israel |
| Currency gains (ILS/USD) | 25% | Israel |

*The exact dividend withholding rate depends on your W-8BEN treaty claim and the dividend type. IBKR applies the treaty rate; reconcile any differences when filing your annual Israeli tax return.

**Important:** IBKR generates detailed annual statements, but these are in US format. You'll need to translate this information into the format required by the Israeli Tax Authority. Many Israeli investors work with a local accountant (רואה חשבון) who handles this annually.

### Currency Conversion - ILS to USD

Israeli investors typically hold ILS and need to convert to USD to trade US markets. IBKR's currency conversion fee structure:

- **0.002% of the converted amount**
- **Minimum: $2.00 per conversion**

On a $10,000 conversion, you pay $2.00 - far lower than most Israeli banks (which typically charge 0.3%-1% on conversions) or many competing brokers. You can hold ILS directly in your IBKR account and convert only when needed.

### Real Shares vs CFDs

IBKR deals exclusively in **real shares** on its main platform. When you buy Apple or SPY through IBKR, you own the actual equity held in custody - not a derivative position against the broker. This is important for Israeli investors from both a legal clarity and a tax reporting perspective. CFDs are treated differently than securities ownership under Israeli law.

### No Hebrew Language Support

IBKR does not offer customer support in Hebrew, and its platform is not available in Hebrew. Support is in English, with some additional European languages. For most Israeli investors with English proficiency this is manageable, but worth knowing upfront.

### Depositing from an Israeli Bank

IBKR accepts international wire transfers via SWIFT. Your Israeli bank will charge an outgoing wire fee on their end (typically 15-30 NIS). IBKR itself does not charge a fee for incoming wire transfers. Processing usually takes 1-3 business days.

**Step 8 - Start trading**

Once approved and funded, you have full access to all markets and features included in your account type.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Can non-US residents use IBKR Lite?

No. IBKR Lite is only available to US residents and is built on payment for order flow (PFOF), which is not permitted in most non-US jurisdictions. All non-US investors receive IBKR Pro accounts automatically, with no option to switch.

### Is Interactive Brokers available in Israel?

Yes. Israeli residents can open IBKR Pro accounts. Israel is not an EU member, so Israeli investors are not subject to the PRIIPs restrictions that prevent EU residents from buying US-listed ETFs. Israelis can buy SPY, QQQ, VTI, and other US-listed ETFs directly through IBKR.

### What is the minimum deposit for Israeli investors?

There is no official minimum deposit. As of 2021, IBKR eliminated the monthly inactivity fee for most accounts. Check current terms at the time of your application, as policies can change.

### Do I need to file taxes in both the US and Israel as an Israeli investor?

You are not a US taxpayer, so you don't file a US tax return. IBKR withholds US [dividend tax](/taxes/taxes-us-dividends-foreign-investors) at source at the treaty rate. You report all investment income and capital gains to the Israeli Tax Authority (רשות המסים) annually. Many Israeli investors use a local tax advisor for this, as the reporting requirements are specific.

### What exactly does the W-8BEN do for Israeli investors?

The W-8BEN certifies to IBKR and the IRS that you are not a US person. It allows you to claim the Israel-US tax treaty dividend withholding rate (15%-25%) instead of the standard 30% withheld from non-US investors without a treaty claim. Without a W-8BEN on file, IBKR would withhold 30% from every US dividend payment.

### Does IBKR offer real shares or CFDs?

Real shares only. Interactive Brokers does not offer CFD products on its main platform. When you buy a stock through IBKR, you own the actual equity held in custody on your behalf.

### Can I hold ILS (Israeli shekel) in my IBKR account?

Yes. IBKR supports multi-currency accounts including ILS. You can hold shekels in your account and convert to USD or other currencies when needed, at a fee of 0.002% (minimum $2.00 per conversion).

### How long does it take to open an IBKR account from Israel?

The online application takes 30-60 minutes to complete. Account approval typically takes 1-3 business days once all required documents (passport and proof of address) are submitted and verified.

### Is IBKR better than eToro for Israeli investors?

They serve different types of investors. eToro offers Hebrew support, a simpler interface, and is better suited for beginners. IBKR Pro is more powerful, costs less for active investors, gives access to real shares across 150+ markets, and pays interest on cash. If you're just starting out, eToro may be the easier entry point. If you're a more serious or active investor, IBKR Pro's cost structure, market access, and features are hard to beat.

### What happens if Interactive Brokers goes bankrupt?

Israeli accounts held under Interactive Brokers LLC (the US entity) are protected by SIPC up to $500,000 in total assets ($250,000 in cash). Client funds are also held segregated from IBKR's own assets by regulation. IBKR is publicly listed, financially transparent, and carries excess capital well above regulatory requirements - making it one of the more financially stable retail brokers operating globally.
