## What Is Saxo Bank?

Saxo Bank is not a retail broker that happens to offer a lot of assets. It is a fully licensed Danish investment bank founded in 1992 - the same regulatory category as a traditional bank, with the capital requirements, oversight, and stability that implies.

This distinction matters. Saxo is designated as a Systemically Important Financial Institution (SIFI) in Denmark, holds an A- credit rating from S&P, and is regulated by top-tier authorities across 15+ jurisdictions. This is not a startup broker or a fintech app. It is a 30-year-old institution.

That background shapes everything about the platform - its depth, its complexity, and its pricing.

## Fees and Costs - The Real Picture

<!-- ADSENSE-RECTANGLE-300x250 -->

### Stock and ETF Trading

Saxo charges a commission on stock and ETF trades, unlike eToro's zero-commission model. For Classic accounts, the minimum on US stocks is $1 per trade. This is not expensive by absolute standards, but it is real compared to zero.

The fee drops significantly at Platinum and VIP. For high-volume investors, Saxo's institutional pricing becomes genuinely competitive.

### The Custody Fee - What Most Reviews Miss

This is the cost that catches investors off guard. Saxo charges an annual custody fee on the value of your holdings:

- Classic: 0.15% per year (minimum €5/month)
- Platinum: 0.12% per year (minimum €5/month)
- VIP: 0.09% per year (minimum €5/month)

**Practical impact:**

| Portfolio Size | Classic (0.15%) | Platinum (0.12%) | VIP (0.09%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| $50,000 | $75/year | - | - |
| $200,000 | $300/year | $240/year | - |
| $500,000 | $750/year | $600/year | - |
| $1,000,000 | $1,500/year | $1,200/year | $900/year |

*Note: Custody fee rates and structure vary by country and Saxo entity. Some jurisdictions have reduced or eliminated the fee. Verify the exact rate for your country before opening an account.*

You can avoid the custody fee entirely by enabling Saxo's stock lending program - Saxo lends out your shares and splits the income 50/50. Many investors activate it to waive the custody fee while still earning extra income. It is a legitimate option, but it carries its own considerations around share recall risk.

### Currency Conversion

Saxo charges ~0.25% on currency conversions. This is significantly better than eToro's 1.5% for ILS accounts, but meaningfully worse than IBKR's near-interbank rate of 0.002%.

| Broker | FX fee on $10,000 |
|---|---|
| IBKR | ~$0.20 |
| Saxo | ~$25 |
| eToro (ILS) | ~$150 |
| Bank transfer | $100-200 |

For investors who convert currency frequently, this difference accumulates. For investors who deposit once and hold, it is a one-time cost.

**No inactivity fee**

Saxo removed its inactivity fee. Previously $100 after 180 days, this charge no longer applies as of 2025. This is a meaningful improvement for long-term buy-and-hold investors.

### For Non-US Investors: What to Verify Before Opening

Saxo is available in 180+ countries. A few points relevant to international investors regardless of jurisdiction:

- **Platform language:** Saxo's interface is in English and select European languages. There is no localized interface for most non-European markets
- **Currency conversion:** ~0.25% from your local currency - verify the exact rate for your currency pair before opening
- **Real shares, not CFDs:** Saxo offers genuine share ownership across its stock and ETF offering, not CFD substitutes
- **Which Saxo entity:** This is the most important check. The regulatory protections, investor compensation scheme, and tax reporting structure differ by which entity onboards you. Verify this before depositing
- **US estate tax:** Non-US investors holding US-domiciled ETFs and stocks may be exposed to US estate tax on assets above ~$60,000. This applies at Saxo as at any broker. Consult a tax advisor for large positions
- **W-8BEN:** Saxo collects this during account setup. Ensure it is submitted and reflects your correct country of tax residency

*Stock Lending: Saxo lends your shares and splits income 50/50 with you. This can offset or eliminate the custody fee. Amounts are estimates - actual offset depends on securities held and market demand for lending.*

*All figures are estimates based on published standard rates. Actual costs vary by country, Saxo entity, and trading volume. IBKR estimate uses standard commission of $0.35/trade minimum.*

*With full Stock Lending activation, Saxo Classic and Platinum costs can drop significantly closer to IBKR in many cases - depending on the securities held and market lending demand.*

**Key takeaway:** IBKR is cheaper on pure cost at every portfolio size. The gap at $50,000 is significant. At $200,000+ with Stock Lending enabled, Saxo becomes more competitive - but IBKR still wins on cost alone. Saxo's advantage is research quality, platform depth, bond access, and institutional infrastructure.

**Saxo makes sense if:**

## When Two Accounts Make Sense

Many serious international investors use both IBKR and Saxo - not as a compromise, but as a deliberate strategy.

**The typical split:**

| Task | Platform | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Active trading, options, FX conversion | IBKR | Lowest cost, best execution |
| Research and analysis | Saxo | Institutional research, SaxoTraderPRO |
| Bond portfolio | Saxo | Superior selection and interface |
| Long-term equity holdings | Either | Depends on custody fee vs FX situation |

**This makes sense if:**
- Your portfolio is $200,000+ and you genuinely use research tools
- You want institutional-grade analysis without paying a wealth manager
- You trade frequently on IBKR but want Saxo's platform for strategic decisions

**This is overkill if:**
- Your portfolio is below $100,000
- You invest passively and rarely check the platform
- You do not use bonds or institutional research

The two-account approach is not unusual among serious investors. It reflects that no single broker wins on every dimension - and that optimizing for cost on one side and capability on the other is a rational decision.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the minimum deposit for Saxo Bank?**
Classic accounts have no minimum deposit in most regions. Platinum requires $200,000 and VIP requires $1,000,000. Account tiers upgrade automatically based on portfolio value.

**Does Saxo charge a custody fee?**
Yes - 0.15% per year for Classic accounts (minimum €5/month), 0.12% for Platinum, and 0.09% for VIP. You can avoid this by enabling the stock lending program.

**How does Saxo compare to IBKR on fees?**
IBKR is cheaper on FX conversion (0.002% vs 0.25%), charges no custody fee, and has lower options commissions. Saxo wins on research quality, platform depth, bond access, and institutional infrastructure. For large portfolios where research and multi-asset access matter, Saxo becomes competitive at Platinum tier.

**Can I trade options on Saxo?**
Yes - real listed options across 1,200+ instruments. This is genuine options trading, not CFD options.

**Is Saxo available in Israel?**
Yes. Israeli investors receive real shares (not CFDs). There is no Hebrew interface. Currency conversion from ILS is ~0.25%.

**What happens if Saxo goes bankrupt?**
Client funds are held separately from company assets. The specific investor compensation scheme depends on which Saxo entity holds your account - this varies by country of residence. Check before opening.

**Does Saxo pay interest on cash?**
This varies by jurisdiction and account type. Check with your specific Saxo entity for current rates.

**What is the inactivity fee?**
Saxo removed its inactivity fee in 2025. It no longer applies.
